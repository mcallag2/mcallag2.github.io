Ursa Minor

The Ursa Minor constellation is found in the norther sky, and its translated name is ‘the lesser bear’. It is situated right next to the Ursa Major constellation, and it contains the Little Dipper. The Ursa Minor constellation contains Polaris also known as the North Star. In its myth, the constellation represents Arcas who is the son of Zeus and the nymph Callisto. Arcas was cast into the sky along with his mother who is represented by the neighboring Ursa Major constellation to prevent their tragic deaths
